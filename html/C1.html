  • A 1571-1858 Records of the Alienation Office

    Surviving records of the Alienation Office, which developed from 1576 to prevent the alienation of feudal land without licence, recording the Alienation Office's revenue- gathering activities.

    These include accounts of the office in A 1, yearly accounts of the receivers general in A 5, and books of accounts of pre- and post fines in A 6. Accounts of fees and emoluments for are in A 3

    Entry books of licences and pardons for alienation are in A 4, entry books of writs of entry are in A 9, and writs of covenant are in A 7

    Master of Chancery's affidavits are in A 3. 'Nil Books' are in A 8

    Office expenses are in A 2
